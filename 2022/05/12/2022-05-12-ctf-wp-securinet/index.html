I took part in Securinet CTF 2022 Final yesterday (didn’t get a problem solved though). The problems are good and I’ve tried to solve them all afterwards.
Here I just write them with some problems from Securinet CTF 2022 Quals.

Securinets 2022 Quals

Sherry(re)

Solution

Solving DLP on a polynomial quotient ring mod N^2.

Knowing $G$ and $G^{secret} \pmod{N^2}$, we can give $(g,s)$ 6 times to guess the secret. And the server will give $share=g^{secret} + \epsilon \pmod{N^2}$ if $g^s \neq G^{secret}$

Actually it’s not a real DLP. If we choose $g=N+1$, and we will get:

$$
(N+1)^{secret}+\epsilon = 1+secret*N+\epsilon \pmod{N^2}
$$

Consider $N,\epsilon$ as vectors mod p, and we’ll find it a SVP, while $\epsilon$ is relatively smaller than others. Our target is to find $secret$ satisfying $\epsilon =secret * N-share-k * p$ is short.

Construct the matrix as follows:

$$
\left[\begin{matrix}share_0&share_1&…&share_i\\ N_0&N_1&…&N_i\\ p \\ &p\\&&….\\&&&p \end{matrix}\right]
$$

Using LLL algorithm we can get $\epsilon$ and get the secret.

Yolida mentioned in his blog that with more shares we can get more equations about secret, and I haven’t considered about it.

Securinets 2022 Final

Yor Forger

Solution

Problem about Shamir Secret Sharing. Given 4 trusted points, we need to give another legal point to reconstruct the right polynomial.

We can find the secret is int(hash(“gimme flag”), 16), which is the zero point of the polynomial. Thus we’ve got 5 points and we can reconstruct the polynomial, after that we can just choose a random point as the payload.
